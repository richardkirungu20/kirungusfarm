who's on the farm

Five ornamental breeds, a growing rabbitry, and peacocks on the way.

Brahma

A gentle giant with heavily feathered legs down to the toes. Calm, cold-hardy, and hard to miss in the yard.

Chicks, eggs & mature birds

Orpington

Round, plush, and famously friendly. A reliable dual-purpose bird that lays well and dresses out heavy.

Chicks, eggs & meat

Silkie

Fluffy, hair-like plumage and a calm temperament. The one guests always want to hold first.

Chicks & hatching eggs

Polish

Famous for the dramatic burst of feathers on top of the head. Striking in a show pen, comical in a rainstorm.

Breeding pairs

Sebright

A true bantam with tight, laced feather patterning and an upright tail. One of the oldest ornamental breeds still kept.

Trios & show stock

Rabbits

A small, growing rabbitry alongside the birds. Kits and mature does and bucks, raised for pets, breeding, or meat.

Kits, bunnies & mature stock

Peacocks

Next on the list. We're building out pens now for our first breeding pair, with chicks to follow.
